# Zero-Friction Ingress and Meaningful Classification

Knowledge work moves fastest when capture friction is minimized and classification is treated as an act of understanding, not administration.

## Principle

Remove all barriers between thought and capture. Every idea, observation, or fragment should have a direct and effortless path into the vault. Organize later, when understanding exists.

Classification should not be an immediate filing decision but a reflective process of discovering what a thought means and where it belongs in the conceptual network.

## Behavior

1. **Zero-Friction Capture** — Thoughts go directly into the inbox, without deciding where or how they fit.
2. **Deferred Classification** — Upon review, classification enriches notes with meaning, tags, and links; it is treated as knowledge formation.
3. **Minimal Initial Metadata** — Require as little structure as possible at capture time.
4. **Iterative Refinement** — Revisiting a note should progressively reveal its relationships to others, moving it toward permanence or deletion.

## Triggers

Apply this concept whenever:
- Capturing spontaneous thoughts, insights, or observations.
- Writing reflective or exploratory notes.
- Converting informal chats, conversations, or context logs into durable knowledge.

## Example Workflow

1. Note enters **inbox** with no friction.
2. On review, assign frontmatter metadata such as `status`, `type`, and `tags`.
3. Link related notes to form semantic relationships.
4. Promote from `inbox` → `scaffolding` → `permanent` status once stable.

## Rationale

Attempting to structure too early obscures thinking with taxonomy. Delay structure until comprehension crystallizes. This allows thought velocity to remain high while still converging on meaningful organization.

## Anti-Pattern

Premature classification, excessive metadata requirements, or rigid folder hierarchies that discourage spontaneous capture.

## Applicability

Applies to personal knowledge management systems, agentic memory architectures, and collaborative vaults where information evolves through revision rather than one-time filing.
